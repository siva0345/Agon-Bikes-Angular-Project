Engine: The Dominar 250 is powered by a 248.77cc, single-cylinder, liquid-cooled, fuel-injected engine. This engine is capable of producing around 27 horsepower and 23.5 Nm of torque.

Performance: Despite being a smaller displacement motorcycle compared to the Dominar 400, the Dominar 250 offers adequate performance for city commuting and highway cruising. It has a top speed of around 130-135 km/h, making it suitable for highway rides.

Design: The Dominar 250 shares its design language with its larger sibling, the Dominar 400. It features a muscular and aggressive styling, with a full-LED lighting system, twin-barrel exhaust, and a sporty stance. The bike has a comfortable riding posture suitable for long-distance touring.

Features:Bajaj has equipped the Dominar 250 with a host of features, including a fully digital instrument cluster with information such as speedometer, tachometer, fuel gauge, odometer, and gear position indicator. It also features a split-seat design, alloy wheels, and a generous fuel tank capacity for long-distance rides.

Suspension and Brakes: The Dominar 250 comes equipped with a USD (Upside Down) front fork and a rear monoshock suspension setup, providing a balanced ride quality. For braking, it features disc brakes at both ends with dual-channel ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) for added safety.

Price: The Dominar 250 is positioned as a mid-range sports tourer and is available at a relatively competitive price point compared to its competitors in the market.

Overall, the Bajaj Dominar 250 offers a blend of performance, comfort, and features, making it a compelling option for riders looking for a versatile motorcycle for daily commuting as well as long-distance touring.
